Websites are considered “places of public accommodation” under Title III of the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA). Websites with inaccessible components can be seen as discriminatory against persons with disabilities, in violation of Title III of the ADA. Examples of website accessibility features:

  • Semantic HTML
  • Alternative text for images
  • Keyboard navigation
  • Proper font sizing
  • Color contrast ratios

With the ADA being a strict liability law with no excuses we work to maintain compliance with Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.0 Level AA success criteria. However, these guidelines are regularly updated. This is the reason we came up with our development + SEO package.
